Jim Cummings

James Jonah Cummings (born November 3, 1952) is an American voice actor and singer, who has appeared in almost 400 roles. He is known for voicing the title character from "Darkwing Duck\ref{Darkwing_Duck}", Dr. Robotnik\ref{Dr._Robotnik} from "Sonic the Hedgehog\ref{Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(TV_series)}", and Pete\ref{Pete_(Disney)}. His other characters include Winnie the Pooh\ref{Winnie_the_Pooh}, Tigger\ref{Tigger}, and the Tasmanian Devil\ref{Tasmanian_Devil_(Looney_Tunes)}. He has performed in numerous Disney\ref{Walt_Disney_Animation_Studios} and DreamWorks\ref{DreamWorks_Animation} animations including "Aladdin\ref{Aladdin_(1992_Disney_film)}", "The Lion King\ref{The_Lion_King}", "Balto\ref{Balto_(film)}", "Antz\ref{Antz}", "The Road to El Dorado\ref{The_Road_to_El_Dorado}", "Shrek\ref{Shrek}", and "The Princess and the Frog\ref{The_Princess_and_the_Frog}". He has also provided voice-over work for video games, such as "Icewind Dale\ref{Icewind_Dale}", "Fallout\ref{Fallout_(video_game)}", "", "Baldur's Gate\ref{Baldur's_Gate}", "Mass Effect 2\ref{Mass_Effect_2}", "", "", "", and "Splatterhouse\ref{Splatterhouse_(2010_video_game)}".

Some of Cummings' earliest vocal work was at Disney, where he replaced Hal Smith\ref{Hal_Smith_(actor)} as the voice of Winnie-the-Pooh\ref{Winnie_the_Pooh_(character)} in 1988 (Smith had taken over the role from longtime actor Sterling Holloway\ref{Sterling_Holloway} in 1981). He began voicing Tigger\ref{Tigger} in 1989, taking over for Paul Winchell\ref{Paul_Winchell} (though Winchell voiced Tigger four more times, the last time being in February 1999) after Winchell died in 2005. In 1991, he ventured to Warner Bros. Animation\ref{Warner_Bros._Animation} and began voicing Tasmanian Devil\ref{Tasmanian_Devil_(Looney_Tunes)} on the animated series "Taz-Mania\ref{Taz-Mania}" (he would voice the character on other Warner Brothers shows, such as "Animaniacs\ref{Animaniacs}", and reprise the voice in 2006 in "Bah, Humduck! A Looney Tunes Christmas\ref{Bah,_Humduck!_A_Looney_Tunes_Christmas}").

When actor Jeremy Irons\ref{Jeremy_Irons}, the voice of Scar\ref{List_of_The_Lion_King_characters_Scar} in "The Lion King\ref{The_Lion_King}", developed vocal problems during recording of the song "Be Prepared\ref{Be_Prepared_(song)}", Cummings was chosen to replace him for the rest of the song. Cummings would later be hired as the singing double for Russell Means in "Pocahontas"\ref{Pocahontas_(1995_film)} and Christopher Lloyd in "Anastasia"\ref{Anastasia_(1997_film)}.

Born in Youngstown, Ohio\ref{Youngstown,_Ohio}, Cummings relocated to New Orleans\ref{New_Orleans}, where he designed and painted Mardi Gras\ref{Mardi_Gras} floats, worked as a river boat deck hand and sang and played drums in the regionally successful rock band, "FUSION". He attended Immaculate Conception and St. Columba\ref{St._Columba_Cathedral_(Youngstown,_Ohio)} grade schools as well as Ursuline High School\ref{Ursuline_High_School_(Youngstown,_Ohio)} and graduated from there in 1970.

He later married and moved to Anaheim, California\ref{Anaheim,_California}, where he managed a video store in the early 1980s, before launching his voice-acting career in late 1984. Cummings has two daughters with Stephanie Cummings, as well as two older children from a previous marriage.
