Lewiston Maineiacs

The Lewiston Maineiacs were a junior ice hockey\ref{junior_ice_hockey} team of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League\ref{Quebec_Major_Junior_Hockey_League} based in Lewiston, Maine\ref{Lewiston,_Maine}. The team played its home games at the Androscoggin Bank Colisée\ref{Androscoggin_Bank_Colisée}. They were the second QMJHL team in the United States\ref{United_States}, and the only one to play a full season. They won the President's Cup\ref{President's_Cup_(QMJHL)} in 2007.

On May 31, 2011, the QMJHL decided to fold the team following eight years of financial losses.

Their franchise was granted for the 1969–70 season. They played in Trois-Rivières\ref{Trois-Rivières}, Quebec\ref{Quebec}, from 1969 to 1992. They were known as the Ducs\ref{Trois-Rivières_Ducs} from 1969 to 1973, and as the Draveurs\ref{Trois-Rivières_Draveurs} from 1973 to 1992. They moved to Sherbrooke\ref{Sherbrooke}, Quebec, in 1992 to become the Faucons (1992–1998) and the Castors\ref{Sherbrooke_Castors} (1998–2003). The Castors moved to Lewiston, Maine\ref{Lewiston,_Maine}, in 2003.

The MAINEiacs clinched the Jean Rougeau Trophy\ref{Jean_Rougeau_Trophy} for first overall in the regular season for 2006–07\ref{2006–07_QMJHL_season}. During the same season, Lewiston increased its annual attendance totals to 94,903 tickets sold. \ref{leagueatt.cgi} In the playoffs that year, Lewiston went 16–1 to cruise to their first President's Cup\ref{President's_Cup_(QMJHL)}.

After winning the 2007 President's Cup, the MAINEiacs won eligibility to play in the 2007 Memorial Cup\ref{2007_Memorial_Cup} in Vancouver\ref{Vancouver}, British Columbia\ref{British_Columbia}. Following an opening win over the Medicine Hat Tigers\ref{Medicine_Hat_Tigers}, the MAINEiacs suffered a huge loss with the injury of their captain, Marc-André Cliche\ref{Marc-André_Cliche}, and never really found their scoring touch. They crashed out of the tournament in fourth place after losing consecutive games to the Vancouver Giants\ref{Vancouver_Giants}, Plymouth Whalers\ref{Plymouth_Whalers}, and the Whalers again in the tie-breaker, scoring only three goals in three games in the process.

On January 25, 2009, it was announced that the MAINEiacs were negotiating with the city of Boisbriand, Quebec\ref{Boisbriand,_Quebec} (a suburb of Montreal\ref{Montreal}), in hopes of relocating the franchise for the 2009–10 season. Under the plan, Mark Just would remain majority owner with 51% of the shares, with former NHL defenseman Joel Bouchard owning the rest. On January 31, the team announced that they had filed their intent to relocate, and had submitted papers to do so to the QMJHL.

In a January 31 media release announcing the move of the MAINEiacs franchise, owner Mark Just said that community support for the team was a major consideration. During the six seasons since the team moved from Sherbrooke\ref{Sherbrooke}, Quebec, to Lewiston, the MAINEiacs never managed to break even. However, on February 2, Farrel Miller, owner of the QMJHL's Montreal Junior Hockey Club\ref{Montreal_Junior_Hockey_Club}, issued a statement that his team would fight the MAINEiacs move to Boisbriand on territorial grounds.

After several weeks of negotiations with a number of Canadian cities (most notably Fredericton\ref{Fredericton}, New Brunswick\ref{New_Brunswick}), MAINEiacs Head Coach Don Macadam announced on March 24, 2009, that the MAINEiacs would be remaining in Lewiston. In June 2010, Lewiston businessman Paul Spellman became minority owner of the team, and in early August 2010, former Moncton Wildcats\ref{Moncton_Wildcats} General Manager Bill Schurman was named as the team's Sports Management Consultant. However, relocation rumors continued to follow the team, with Summerside, Prince Edward Island\ref{Summerside,_Prince_Edward_Island} (where Schurman, a native of that town, had recently been the municipality's Director of Community Services), and St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador\ref{St._John's,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador}, named as a potential destinations in January 2011.

Schurman's denials of the team looking into a Summerside relocation proved unconvincing, and the rumours had a negative effect on attendance. However, Schurman's dream of moving the MAINEiacs to his home city ultimately failed, as a preliminary exploration to gauge support for a move to Summerside found little traction. The team then began to look at other possibilities, but found that few other relocation options existed.

The Lewiston franchise was disbanded by the QMJHL in the summer of 2011. To replace the defunct MAINEiacs club, the Sherbrooke Phoenix is scheduled to begin play for the 2012–13 season. It will be the 3rd franchise to call Sherbrooke home in the last 25 years.

On May 31, 2011, the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League held a conference call to prepare an offer to purchase the team from Mark Just for 3.5 million dollars. Under the league's plan, the MAINEiacs would then be disbanded, with players being selected by other clubs in a dispersal draft. Late on May 31, the league issued a press release confirming the sale of the MAINEiacs to the league, and the subsequent disbanding / dispersal draft occurred the following week. While Androscoggin Bank Colisee owners have stated that they are searching for a replacement team from outside of the QMJHL to take the MAINEiacs place, they have as yet been unable to do so.

The City of Lewiston and the Androscoggin Bank Colisee would end up getting the American Hockey League's Portland Pirates to move to the Colisee for one season after a Lawsuit and Breakdown in discussions of their lease with the Cumberland County Civic Center in Portland.
