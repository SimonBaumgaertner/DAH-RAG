Dictyosperma

Dictyosperma is a monotypic\ref{monotypic} genus\ref{genus} of flowering plant\ref{flowering_plant} in the palm\ref{arecaceae} family found in the Mascarene Islands\ref{Mascarene_Islands} in the Indian Ocean\ref{Indian_Ocean} (Mauritius\ref{Mauritius}, Réunion\ref{Réunion} and Rodrigues\ref{Rodrigues_(island)}). The sole species\ref{species}, Dictyosperma album, is widely cultivated in the tropics\ref{tropics} but has been farmed to near extinction\ref{extinction} in its native habitat. It is commonly called princess palm or hurricane palm, the latter owing to its ability to withstand strong winds by easily shedding leaves. It is closely related to, and resembles, palms in the "Archontophoenix\ref{Archontophoenix}" genus. The genus is named from two Greek\ref{Greek_language} words meaning "net" and "seed" and the epithet is Latin\ref{Latin} for "white", the common color of the crownshaft at the top of the trunk.

The ringed trunks are solitary at 15 cm in diameter with a slight bulge at the base, occasionally reaching up to 12 m in height. The crownshaft\ref{crownshaft} is over a meter tall, swollen at the base, and covered in white wax— which has given the palm its epithet "album"— and small, matted brown hairs, producing a rounded leaf crown 4.5 m wide and 3 m tall. The 2.5 to 3 m leaves\ref{leaf} are borne on short 30 cm petioles; the arching leaflets are pointed at the apice, from 60 – 90 cm, dark green in color, and emerge from the rachis in a single plane.

On flowering, they produce up to six inflorescence\ref{inflorescence}s which ring the trunk below the crownshaft, branched to one order, with white to yellow flower\ref{flower}s, which are male and female\ref{Dioecious}; both pistillate and staminate flowers have three sepals and three petal\ref{petal}s, the former being smaller than the latter. The ovoid fruit\ref{fruit} ripen to purple or black in color, containing one brown, ellipsoidal seed\ref{seed}.
