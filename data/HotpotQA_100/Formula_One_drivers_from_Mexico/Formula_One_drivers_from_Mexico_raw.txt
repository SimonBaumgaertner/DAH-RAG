Formula One drivers from Mexico

There have been six Formula One drivers from Mexico who have taken part in races since the championship began in 1950. Pedro Rodríguez\ref{Pedro_Rodríguez_(racing_driver)} is the most successful Mexican\ref{Mexico} driver being the only one to have won a grand prix. Sergio Pérez\ref{Sergio_Pérez}, the only other Mexican to finish on the podium, currently races with Sahara Force India F1 Team\ref{Force_India} .

Pedro Rodríguez won two races out of the 54 he started and is the only Mexican driver to finish in first position.

Sergio Pérez\ref{Sergio_Pérez} began his career in with Sauber\ref{Sauber} before moving to McLaren\ref{McLaren} in and then Force India\ref{Force_India} in .

Brothers Pedro and Ricardo Rodríguez\ref{Ricardo_Rodríguez_(racing_driver)} raced in the 1960s. Héctor Rebaque\ref{Héctor_Rebaque} raced in the 1970s, competing in more events than any other Mexican driver. Moisés Solana\ref{Moisés_Solana} attended races in America and Mexico throughout the 1960s.

Esteban Gutiérrez\ref{Esteban_Gutiérrez} drove for Sauber\ref{Sauber} in and and after a one year gap in which he was a Ferrari Test Driver came back to drive for Haas F1 Team\ref{Haas_F1_Team} in 2016.
