America East Conference

The America East Conference is a collegiate athletic conference\ref{List_of_NCAA_conferences} affiliated with the NCAA\ref{National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association} Division I\ref{NCAA_Division_I}, whose members are located mainly in the Northeastern\ref{Northeastern_United_States} United States\ref{United_States}. The conference was known as the Eastern College Athletic Conference-North\ref{Eastern_College_Athletic_Conference} from 1979 to 1988 and the North Atlantic Conference from 1988 to 1996.

The America East Conference was founded as the Eastern College Athletic Conference-North, a men's basketball-only athletic conference in 1979. The charter members were the University of Rhode Island\ref{University_of_Rhode_Island}, the College of the Holy Cross\ref{College_of_the_Holy_Cross}, Canisius College\ref{Canisius_College}, Niagara University\ref{Niagara_University}, Colgate University\ref{Colgate_University}, Northeastern University\ref{Northeastern_University}, Boston University\ref{Boston_University}, the University of Maine\ref{University_of_Maine}, the University of New Hampshire\ref{University_of_New_Hampshire} and the University of Vermont\ref{University_of_Vermont}. There are currently five schools with associate membership. Four schools from California\ref{California} moved their field hockey teams into the America East in July 2015. The newest associate member is the Virginia\ref{Virginia} Military Institute\ref{Virginia_Military_Institute} (VMI), which joined in men's and women's swimming & diving starting with the 2017–18 school year.

Three schools have had single-sport membership in the past. Two of these, Fairfield and Providence, moved their America East sports into their all-sports conferences. The third, NJIT, left when it joined a conference that sponsored its America East sport.

The America East Conference sponsors championship competition in eight men's and ten women's NCAA sanctioned sports. The most recent changes to the roster of America East sports were announced in 2016, with the dropping of women's tennis after the 2015–16 season due to a lack of sponsoring teams and the revival of men's swimming and diving effective in the 2017–18 school year.

The conference does not sponsor football. Four members have football programs, all of which compete in the Colonial Athletic Association\ref{Colonial_Athletic_Association}: Albany, Maine, New Hampshire, and Stony Brook.

The conference does not sponsor ice hockey\ref{ice_hockey}. Members who participate in that sport (UMass Lowell for men only, and Maine, New Hampshire, and Vermont for both men and women) do so as members of Hockey East\ref{Hockey_East}.
