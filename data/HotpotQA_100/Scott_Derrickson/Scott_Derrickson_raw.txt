Scott Derrickson

Scott Derrickson (born July 16, 1966) is an American director, screenwriter and producer. He lives in Los Angeles\ref{Los_Angeles}, California\ref{California}. He is best known for directing horror film\ref{horror_film}s such as "Sinister\ref{Sinister_(film)}", "The Exorcism of Emily Rose\ref{The_Exorcism_of_Emily_Rose}", and "Deliver Us From Evil\ref{Deliver_Us_from_Evil_(2014_film)}", as well as the 2016 Marvel Cinematic Universe\ref{Marvel_Cinematic_Universe} installment, "Doctor Strange\ref{Doctor_Strange_(2016_film)}."

Derrickson grew up in Denver, Colorado\ref{Denver,_Colorado}. He graduated from Biola University\ref{Biola_University} with a B.A.\ref{Bachelor_of_Arts} in Humanities\ref{Humanities}, with an emphasis on literature and philosophy, and a B.A. in communications\ref{communications}, with an emphasis on film, and a minor in theological studies\ref{theology}. He earned his M.A.\ref{Master_of_Arts} in film production from USC School of Cinematic Arts\ref{USC_School_of_Cinematic_Arts}.

Derrickson co-wrote and directed the film "The Exorcism of Emily Rose\ref{The_Exorcism_of_Emily_Rose}" which was loosely based on a true story about Anneliese Michel\ref{Anneliese_Michel}. The film won the 2005 Saturn Award for Best Horror or Thriller Film\ref{Saturn_Award_for_Best_Horror_or_Thriller_Film} and in 2006 was named in the Chicago Film Critics Association\ref{Chicago_Film_Critics_Association}'s list of the "Top 100 Scariest Films Ever Made." Theatrical box office gross for "The Exorcism of Emily Rose" was over $144 million worldwide. That same year, Derrickson wrote "Land of Plenty\ref{Land_of_Plenty}" for director Wim Wenders\ref{Wim_Wenders}, an independent drama starring Michelle Williams\ref{Michelle_Williams_(actress)}. Derrickson next directed "The Day the Earth Stood Still\ref{The_Day_the_Earth_Stood_Still_(2008_film)}" starring Keanu Reeves\ref{Keanu_Reeves} and Jennifer Connelly\ref{Jennifer_Connelly}, written by David Scarpa\ref{David_Scarpa}. The film was released in late 2008 and earned over $233 million worldwide.

In August 2011, Derrickson teamed up with producer Jason Blum\ref{Jason_Blum} to write and direct "Sinister\ref{Sinister_(film)}", a mystery-horror film starring Ethan Hawke\ref{Ethan_Hawke}. The $3 million picture was released in theaters by Summit Entertainment on October 12, 2012 and received generally positive critical reviews. "Sinister" earned over $48 million at the U.S. box office and over $78 million worldwide. Derrickson wrote but did not direct "Sinister 2\ref{Sinister_2}".

Derrickson's script "Devil's Knot\ref{Devil's_Knot_(film)}" was directed by Atom Egoyan\ref{Atom_Egoyan}. The film had its world premiere at the 2013 Toronto International Film Festival\ref{Toronto_International_Film_Festival}. It was released in 2014. Derrickson directed his own script, "Deliver Us from Evil\ref{Deliver_Us_from_Evil_(2014_film)}", for producer Jerry Bruckheimer\ref{Jerry_Bruckheimer} and Sony Screen Gems\ref{Sony_Pictures_Television_Screen_Gems_to_Sony_Pictures_Television}. Eric Bana\ref{Eric_Bana} played the lead role, and the film was released wide in theaters on July 2, 2014.

In 2014, Derrickson wrote a film version of "The Outer Limits\ref{The_Outer_Limits_(1963_TV_series)}" with Cargill. Other upcoming Derrickson projects include an adaptation of Stephen King\ref{Stephen_King}'s "The Breathing Method\ref{The_Breathing_Method}" with Jason Blum\ref{Jason_Blum} producing, and an adaptation of the popular video game "" for CBS Films\ref{CBS_Films}.

Derrickson directed the film "Doctor Strange\ref{Doctor_Strange_(2016_film)}", based on the Marvel Comics\ref{Marvel_Comics} property\ref{Doctor_Strange} and part of the Marvel Cinematic Universe\ref{Marvel_Cinematic_Universe}. It was released in November 2016.

In 2017, it was announced that he would direct Josh Friedman's TNT pilot "Snowpiercer" starring Jennifer Connelly.

Derrickson married a nurse in September 1993 and has two children. He is a follower of Christianity.
