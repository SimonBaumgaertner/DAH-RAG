Elko Regional Airport

Elko Regional Airport (IATA\ref{International_Air_Transport_Association_airport_code}: EKO, ICAO\ref{International_Civil_Aviation_Organization_airport_code}: KEKO, FAA\ref{Federal_Aviation_Administration} LID\ref{Location_identifier}: EKO) , formerly Elko Municipal Airport, is a mile west of downtown Elko\ref{Elko,_Nevada}, in Elko County\ref{Elko_County,_Nevada}, Nevada\ref{Nevada}.

The airport was named J.C. Harris Field in 1975 in honor of Jess C. Harris, a sheriff from Elko known as "The Flying Sheriff".

On April 6, 1926, when it was called Elko Airport, the airfield was the terminus for the first scheduled air mail\ref{air_mail} run in the United States, flown by Varney Air Lines\ref{Varney_Air_Lines}. Varney was a predecessor of United Air Lines\ref{United_Air_Lines}.

As early as 1931, Elko was stop on a passenger service flight between New York City\ref{New_York_City} and San Francisco\ref{San_Francisco}. In the June 15, 1931 timetable United Airlines\ref{United_Airlines} predecessor National Air Transport flew New York City - Cleveland - Toledo - Chicago, connecting to Boeing Air Transport's flight to Iowa City - Des Moines - Omaha - Lincoln - North Platte - Cheyenne - Rock Springs - Salt Lake City - Elko - Reno - Sacramento - Oakland. Schedule time was 31 hours westbound and 28 hours eastbound. United later served Elko with Boeing 247\ref{Boeing_247}s, Douglas DC-3\ref{Douglas_DC-3}s and Convair 340\ref{Convair_340}s.

Starting in November 1977 United Boeing 737-200\ref{Boeing_737-200} jetliners flew San Francisco - Reno - Elko - Ely - Salt Lake City service and back; in 1982 United ended this flight, which had been previously operated with a Douglas DC-6B\ref{Douglas_DC-6B} aircraft and was United's last piston powered flight. In the April 27, 1969 United timetable a DC-6 was listed as the aircraft type operating San Francisco - Oakland - Reno - Elko - Ely - Salt Lake City service; the return trip skipped Oakland. This was the only piston flight in the timetable at the time. In 1970 United replaced the DC-6 service with Convair 580\ref{Convair_580} turboprops operated by Frontier Airlines (1950-1986)\ref{Frontier_Airlines_(1950-1986)} via a subcontract arrangement; the flights used the "UA" airline code until 737s took over.

Casino Express Airlines\ref{Casino_Express_Airlines} was based in Elko and supported the local casino industry, flying scheduled charter Boeing 737-200\ref{Boeing_737-200}s from Elko to many cities starting in the late 1980s. In 1994, Casino Express scheduled weekend only flights nonstop between Elko and Portland, OR and Seattle with McDonnell Douglas MD-80\ref{McDonnell_Douglas_MD-80}s. Casino Express eventually changed its name to Xtra Airways\ref{Xtra_Airways} and later ended jet service to Elko.

Another carrier that operated jets to Elko was Royal West Airlines flying BAe 146-200\ref{BAe_146-200}s nonstop to Las Vegas in 1987.

SkyWest Airlines\ref{SkyWest_Airlines} began serving Elko as an independent commuter carrier during the early 1980s, flying Swearingen Metro\ref{Swearingen_Metro}s to Reno, Salt Lake City and Ely. By the late 1980s SkyWest was serving the airport as Western Express for Western Airlines\ref{Western_Airlines} via code sharing\ref{code_sharing} agreement with flights mainly to Salt Lake City on Metros. Following the merger of Western into Delta Air Lines\ref{Delta_Air_Lines}, SkyWest became a Delta Connection\ref{Delta_Connection} carrier via a code sharing agreement and in 1989 was flying Embraer EMB-120 Brasilia\ref{Embraer_EMB-120_Brasilia}s to Salt Lake City in addition to Metro service. SkyWest continues to serve the airport as a Delta Connection carrier, the only airline serving Elko.

Elko Regional Airport covers 700 acre and has two asphalt\ref{asphalt} runway\ref{runway}s: 5/23 measuring 7,214 x 150 ft (2,199 x 46 m) and 12/30 measuring 2,871 x 60 ft (875 x 18 m).

In the year ending August 31, 2007 the airport had 16,520 aircraft operations, average 45 per day: 73% general aviation\ref{general_aviation}, 27% air taxi\ref{air_taxi} and helicopter</a>.

SkyWest\ref{SkyWest_Airlines} operating as Delta Connection\ref{Delta_Connection} flies Bombardier CRJ-200\ref{Bombardier_CRJ-200}s between Elko and Delta Air Lines\ref{Delta_Air_Lines}' hub at Salt Lake City International Airport\ref{Salt_Lake_City_International_Airport}.
